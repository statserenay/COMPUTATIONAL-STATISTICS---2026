Section 01

The Core Idea

The Inverse Transform Method is the most fundamental algorithm for generating random variates from a target distribution. The key insight: if you can invert the CDF, you can convert Uniform(0,1) samples into samples from any distribution.

The Probability Integral Transform

Probability Integral Transform — Theorem
If U ~ Uniform(0,1) and F has inverse F⁻¹, then
X = F⁻¹(U) has CDF F(x)

Proof

P(F⁻¹(U) ≤ x) = P(U ≤ F(x)) = F(x) ✓
because P(U ≤ t) = t for t ∈ [0,1] when U ~ Uniform(0,1)
General Algorithm — 5 Steps
  1. Identify target PDF f(x) and integrate to obtain CDF F(x)
  2. Solve for the quantile function: F⁻¹(u) (algebraically invert the CDF)
  3. Generate U ~ Uniform(0,1) using runif(n) in R
  4. Compute X = F⁻¹(U) — these are your target random samples
  5. Verify: compare sample statistics and histogram against the true PDF
ℹ
Why it works: R's runif() produces high-quality U(0,1) pseudo-random numbers. Any smooth invertible CDF maps those uniforms exactly into the target distribution — no rejection, no approximation.
Section 02

Exponential Distribution

Continuous Exponential(λ) f(x) = λ e^{−λx}, x ≥ 0

Step 1 — CDF

F(x) = ∫₀ˣ λ e^{−λt} dt = 1 − e^{−λx}

Step 2 — Invert

u = 1 − e^{−λx} ⟹ e^{−λx} = 1−u ⟹ x = −(1/λ)·ln(1−u)
∴ F⁻¹(u) = −ln(U) / λ (since 1−U ~ U(0,1) also)
✦
Since 1 − U is also Uniform(0,1), we simplify to −ln(U)/λ. Both forms produce the same distribution.

Uniform(a, b) Distribution

Continuous Uniform(a, b) f(x) = 1/(b−a), a ≤ x ≤ b
F(x) = (x−a)/(b−a) ⟹ u(b−a) = x−a
∴ F⁻¹(u) = a + u·(b−a)

Section 04

Weibull Distribution

Reliability Weibull(k, λ) f(x) = (k/λ)(x/λ)^{k−1} e^{−(x/λ)^k}
F(x) = 1 − exp(−(x/λ)^k)
u = 1−exp(−(x/λ)^k) ⟹ (x/λ)^k = −ln(1−u)
∴ F⁻¹(u) = λ·(−ln(1−u))^{1/k}

Shape k > 1: increasing hazard; k = 1: Exponential; k < 1: decreasing hazard.

Cauchy Distribution

Heavy-tailed Cauchy(x₀, γ) f(x) = 1/[πγ(1+((x−x₀)/γ)²)]
F(x) = ½ + (1/π)·arctan((x−x₀)/γ)
π(u−½) = arctan((x−x₀)/γ) ⟹ x−x₀ = γ·tan(π(u−½))
∴ F⁻¹(u) = x₀ + γ·tan(π(u−½))
⚠
No moments exist. Mean and variance are undefined. The sample mean does NOT converge — the Law of Large Numbers fails spectacularly. Always use the median for location.

Discrete Distributions

For discrete distributions the CDF is a step function. The inverse is defined as:

F⁻¹(u) = min{ x : F(x) ≥ u }
